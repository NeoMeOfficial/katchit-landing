how it works

Three movements. One quiet tool.

Katch is built around how thoughts actually arrive — fast and scattered — and how you actually act on them: in the gaps, not on schedule.

01
katch

Drop anything that takes your attention.

A link, a voice note, a photo of a book page, a thought you’d otherwise type into the Notes app and forget. One blank input — the home of the app — quietly holds it all.

  • One tap from the iOS share sheet
  • ⌘N from anywhere on the desktop
  • Voice, photo, paste — all from the same card
  • Forward emails to catch@katchit.app
02
plan

Grouped by project. Sorted by attention.

Each thing lands in your Unassigned bucket with a suggested project, urgency, and due date. Accept the suggestion in one tap or change it in three. Overdue stays visible. Everything else stays quiet.

  • Buckets are the projects you actually have
  • AI suggestions you can override at any time
  • Search by keyword, tag, or bucket
  • The only red on the screen is what’s overdue
03
do

What fits the moment you’re in.

Tell Katch how much time you have, what kind of attention you can give, how urgent — and which project to draw from. It hands back exactly what matches. Not your whole list. The right next thing.

  • Time: 5 / 15 / 30 / 1h+
  • Intent: read · watch · do · ideate
  • Urgency: anytime · soon · today
  • Bucket: all your projects, or one
why it’s different

It asks, before it lists.

Most inbox apps hand you everything and ask you to sort it. Katch asks four small questions — time, intent, urgency, and project — and shows you only what’s left after the answers.

01 · no infinite list

You’ll only see what fits the moment.

The list isn’t the product — the filter is. You never open Katch to a wall of 200 items. You open it to the three that match what you can actually do right now.

02 · no nagging

No streaks, no badges, no red counts.

Empty inboxes are good and silence is a feature. The only red on the page is the one above the i — and the rare urgent dot when something is genuinely overdue.

03 · no setup

The AI does the cataloguing.

Katch suggests the project, the urgency, and the due date based on what you caught. You confirm or change in a tap. There are no required tags, no templates, no rituals.

04 · made carefully

A calm tool, not another system.

Built for people who want a notebook that thinks ahead — not another productivity stack to maintain. Drawn deliberately. Warm paper, near-black ink, one quiet red dot.
